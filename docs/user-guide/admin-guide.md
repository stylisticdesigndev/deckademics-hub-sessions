# Deckademics Admin Guide

This guide covers everything administrators manage in Deckademics. Each section explains **what it is**, **why it matters**, and **how to do it**, step by step.

## Getting Started & Roles

### Logging in as an admin
1. Log in with your admin email and password.
2. You land on the **Admin Dashboard**.

### Switching between admin and teaching views
Admins can also teach. To go back to the instructor side, use **Return to Teaching View** at the top of the admin menu.

### Who can see payroll
Payment and payroll sections are restricted. Only the owner account sees **Student Payments** and **Instructor Payments**. All admins can open the **Payments** sandbox preview for testing.

## Dashboard & Notifications

### Reading the dashboard
The dashboard gives a school-wide overview. Badges and counters highlight anything that needs attention.

### Notification badges
Red badges in the menu flag items that need action — for example pending sign-ups, unread messages, open bug reports, and overdue payments. Numbers update on their own.

## Instructor Management

### Approving and managing instructors
1. Open **Instructors** from the menu. A badge shows how many are pending.
2. Review a pending instructor and **Approve** them.
3. Use the side panel to **Add** a new instructor or **Edit** an existing one.
4. To remove access, **Deactivate** the instructor. Their students are safely unassigned so no one is left without coverage.

## Student Management

### Approving and managing students
1. Open **Students** from the menu. A badge shows how many are pending.
2. Use the **Active / Pending / Inactive** tabs to filter.
3. Open a pending student and **Approve** them.
4. Use the side panel to **Add** a new student or **Edit** an existing one.

## Curriculum Management

### Editing the curriculum
Curriculum content (the rubrics describing what is taught) is kept separate from the trackable **Skills** used for assessment.

1. Open **Curriculum** from the menu.
2. Edit the topics for each level (Novice through Advanced).
3. Remember: editing curriculum text does not change the Skills scoring list — those are managed under **Skills**.

## Skills & Progress Overview

### Managing the skills list
1. Open **Skills** from the menu.
2. Review and maintain the assessable skills (scored 0–100). Advanced is a single open proficiency.

### Monitoring progress school-wide
1. Open **Progress Overview** from the menu.
2. See how students are progressing across the whole school. A student's overall score is the average of all their skill percentages; missing skills count as 0%.

## Attendance

### Viewing school-wide attendance
1. Open **Attendance** from the menu.
2. Review attendance across Classrooms 1, 2, and 3, marked Present (green) or Absent (red).

## Payments

### Student Payments (owner only)
1. Open **Student Payments** from the menu.
2. Set up installment plans and tiered pricing for students.
3. Track who has paid and who is overdue.

### Instructor Payments (owner only)
1. Open **Instructor Payments** from the menu.
2. Review payroll history (shown 10 at a time).
3. Mark payments as paid — paid is a final status.

### Payments sandbox preview
The **Payments** preview is a safe testing area available to all admins for trying out the payment screens without affecting real records.

## Communication

### Messages
1. Open **Messages** from the menu.
2. Read and respond to conversations. Unread counts appear as badges.

### Announcements
1. Open **Announcements** from the menu.
2. Post announcements by category. Students and instructors can mark them read or dismiss them.

## Feedback

### Bug Reports
1. Open **Bug Reports** from the menu. A badge shows open reports.
2. Review what users reported and update the status as you work through them.

### Feature Requests
1. Open **Feature Requests** from the menu.
2. Review requested ideas and update their status (for example open or planned).

## Settings

### Adjusting app settings
1. Open **Settings** from the menu.
2. Update the available configuration options for the school.
## Guided Tours

### First-time walkthrough
On your first sign-in, a short welcome introduces the admin console. Skip any time.

### Per-page tours
The first time you open a page (Instructors, Students, Curriculum, Skills, Payments, etc.), a short tour highlights the main controls. Click **Skip** to dismiss.

### Replaying a tour
1. Open **Profile** from your avatar menu.
2. Scroll to the **App walkthrough** card.
3. Click **Replay welcome**, pick any page tour to replay, or **Reset all tours** so every tour auto-plays again on next visit.
